# AI2 Robotics
_Private | Embodied AI Humanoid_
_Meeting context: TBD — update with meeting details_

## Overview

Founded April 2023 in Shenzhen by Dr. Yandong Eric Guo, AI2 Robotics builds general-purpose humanoid robots (AlphaBot series) powered by its proprietary GOVLA (Global & Omni-body Vision-Language-Action) model, branded Alpha Brain. GOVLA is the world's first model enabling 360° perception, whole-body control, and long-horizon task reasoning — a differentiated end-to-end architecture vs. competitors using third-party foundation models. Targets industrial manufacturing: quality inspection, assembly, and logistics across automotive, electronics, semiconductors, and biotech. Opened its FiS-VLA model (June 2025, endorsed by Yann LeCun). Debuted AlphaBot 2 at the BRIDGE Summit in UAE with live autonomous coffee-making and drum performance demos. Launched ZhiCube modular AI service space concept (Dec 2025) signaling expansion beyond pure industrial into commercial robotics-as-a-service.

## Revenue Drivers
- **AlphaBot Robot Sales & Services**: B2B industrial robot sales to manufacturing (auto, semi, electronics, biotech) with service/maintenance contracts. Deployed at Dongfeng Liuzhou Motor.
- **Data Closed-Loop**: Deployment data feeds back into GOVLA model training — flywheel that deepens AI moat over time.
- **ZhiCube Modular Spaces**: Early-stage commercial robotics-as-a-service in retail/hospitality. New business line launched Dec 2025.
- **Government/Policy Revenue**: Beneficiary of China's national robotics funding (~$50B allocated 2025), government procurement, and industrial subsidies.

## Narrative & Catalysts

The fastest-financing embodied AI firm in China — 12 rounds in a single year culminating in a unicorn Series B. Real factory deployments (not just demos) give credibility vs. vaporware competitors. CEO Eric Guo publicly targeting IPO in 1-2 years. The core bet is that end-to-end GOVLA architecture creates a durable moat vs. competitors assembling from third-party models.

- FiS-VLA open-source model endorsed by Yann LeCun — rare external technical validation
- AlphaBot 2 debuted at BRIDGE Summit with world-first autonomous demos
- 10x production scale-up planned (1K → 10K units) in 2026
- Baidu partnership enhances AI capabilities; CRRC provides manufacturing/testing expertise
- Competition fundraising aggressively — AgiBot, Engine AI, Kepler all raising

## Upside Catalysts
- Production velocity — 10x scale from 1K to 10K units would validate manufacturing capability
- VLA model leadership — GOVLA differentiation in multi-task generalization vs. task-specific competitors
- China policy support — national standards due Q2 2026, government procurement, industrial subsidies
- IPO premium — listing (HK or A-share) would crystallize valuation and provide growth capital

## Downside Risks
- Component supply chain constraints — actuators, sensors, GPUs all under US export control pressure
- Competitive pressure — Unitree shipped 5,500 units in 2025, AgiBot 5,168; both far ahead on volume and preparing IPOs
- Technology risk — VLA models still require significant human oversight for complex tasks; autonomy gap between demos and production
- Valuation risk — 12 rounds in one year at >$1.45B for a company with triple-digit monthly deliveries raises capital efficiency questions

## Sector Trends

China accounts for 87-90% of global humanoid robot shipments. Embodied AI market grew to $4.4B in 2025, projected $23B by 2030 (5x in 5 years). Chinese government committed ~$50B to robotics in 2025 with national standards due Q2 2026. Unitree shipped 36x more units than US rivals. Automotive sector leads industrial adoption with quality inspection as the first killer use case. Both Unitree and AgiBot preparing IPOs in 2026, escalating the capital race.

## Key Questions to Ask

Q1. With 12 rounds in 2025 and Series B at >$1.45B, what is the concrete IPO timeline and preferred venue (HK vs. A-share)? What milestones gate the decision?

Q2. Current run-rate is "triple-digit monthly" — what's the actual number? Can you break down by customer type, industry vertical, and ASPs?

Q3. Unitree shipped 5,500 units and AgiBot 5,168 in 2025 vs. your 1,000. What's the realistic path to closing the volume gap, and does GOVLA justify a premium positioning?

Q4. What are unit economics today vs. at 10K units/year? At what production scale do you reach gross margin breakeven?

Q5. How does GOVLA (Alpha Brain) concretely differentiate from competitors' approaches — specifically, what tasks can your robots do autonomously that others cannot?

Q6. How are you managing supply chain for actuators, sensors, and compute given US export controls? What percentage of critical components are domestically sourced?

Q7. ZhiCube launched Dec 2025 — is this a parallel business line or a future pivot away from industrial? What's the revenue contribution target?
