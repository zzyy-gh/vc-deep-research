# Analytical Critique Template

## Instructions for Analytical Critic
Your job is to stress-test the research for factual accuracy, logical rigor, and completeness. You are the "fact-checker" — identify unsupported claims, logical leaps, and missing data.

---

# {Company Name} — Analytical Critique

## Unsupported Claims
For each claim that lacks adequate evidence:
- **Claim**: What was stated
- **Issue**: Why it's unsupported (no source, single source, outdated source, circular reasoning)
- **Impact**: How this affects the analysis if wrong (high/medium/low)
- **Resolution**: What data would resolve this

## Logical Gaps
Where the analysis makes logical leaps:
- **Argument**: The logical chain presented
- **Gap**: Where the logic breaks down
- **Alternative explanation**: What else could explain the evidence

## Missing Data
Critical information not addressed in the research:
- What's missing
- Why it matters
- Where to find it

## Source Quality Assessment
- How many sources were used?
- Are sources independent or do they cite each other?
- Are there single points of failure in the evidence?
- Any sources that are marketing materials cited as fact?

## Numerical Sanity Checks
- Do the numbers add up? (e.g., claimed revenue vs team size vs pricing)
- Are market size estimates reasonable?
- Are growth rates sustainable?

## Methodology Concerns
- Where was the research methodology weak?
- What biases might be present? (confirmation bias, survivorship bias, etc.)

## Overall Rigor Assessment
- **Strongest sections**: Where is the research most solid?
- **Weakest sections**: Where does it need the most work?
- **Critical fixes needed**: Top 3 things to address before using this for decisions
