# AuraSell -- Research Report

## Executive Summary

AuraSell is an AI-native CRM and go-to-market (GTM) platform founded in 2023 (launched from stealth August 2025) that aims to consolidate 15+ fragmented sales tools into a single intelligent system. The company raised a $30M seed round from N47 (formerly Next47/Siemens Venture Capital), Menlo Ventures, and Unusual Ventures -- a round that was oversubscribed to $40M and closed in 28 hours, signaling strong investor conviction. Co-founded by Jason Eubanks (CEO), a 20-year GTM operator who scaled Harness from $1M to $100M+ ARR, and Srinivas Bandi (CTO), a multi-time engineering leader at VMware, Nutanix, and Meraki, the team has deep domain credibility.

The core thesis is that the enterprise sales stack is broken -- bloated with 15+ point solutions that create data silos, workflow fragmentation, and massive overhead. AuraSell's bet is that an AI-native architecture built from first principles can replace this stack while delivering agentic automation across the full revenue lifecycle. The company reported 41 million agent runs within weeks of launch and has named customers (AVO Automation, MobiClocks) who have replaced Salesforce and multiple point tools. In February 2026, AuraSell pivoted its go-to-market with "GTM OS," an overlay that works atop existing CRMs like Salesforce and HubSpot -- a pragmatic shift that reduces adoption friction.

Primary risks include: competing against Salesforce's $2.9B AI/Data Cloud ARR and its Agentforce platform, unproven unit economics at scale, dependency on AI infrastructure costs, and the challenge of replacing deeply entrenched enterprise systems. The company is pre-revenue or very early revenue with no disclosed ARR figures.

## Product

**What it does**: AuraSell is a unified platform that replaces CRM (Salesforce), sales engagement (Outreach/Salesloft), conversation intelligence (Gong/Chorus), data enrichment (ZoomInfo/Apollo), CPQ, territory management, forecasting, and coaching tools with a single AI-native system. The platform uses agentic AI workflows where AI agents execute tasks (research, enrichment, qualification, outreach, record updates) alongside human sellers.

**Two deployment models** (as of Feb 2026):
1. **Standalone CRM**: Full Salesforce replacement with native AI throughout
2. **GTM OS**: AI-native overlay that sits atop existing Salesforce/HubSpot installations, adding automation without requiring CRM migration

The GTM OS pivot is strategically significant -- it addresses the biggest objection ("we can't rip out Salesforce") while still capturing wallet share and establishing a beachhead for eventual full migration.

**Core capabilities**:
- AI-powered data enrichment with natural language commands
- Automated ICP and persona management across 85M accounts and 870M contacts
- AI deal reviews, coaching, and forecasting
- Agentic prospecting and sequence automation
- AI conversation intelligence and native voice dialer
- Native CPQ embedded in the platform
- 200+ integrations with GTM, ERP, and productivity tools
- Custom agent builder (no-code, upcoming)

**Product maturity**: Generally available (GA) since August 2025. Feature velocity appears high -- CPQ, voice dialer, and 200+ integrations shipped within three months of launch. GTM OS launched February 2026. This pace suggests the two years in stealth (2023-2025) were used for substantial product development.

**Target buyer**: Mid-market to enterprise CROs, VP Sales, RevOps leaders. Initial traction appears stronger in mid-market (AVO Automation, MobiClocks are sub-enterprise).

## Market

**Problem**: Enterprise sales teams use 15+ disconnected tools (CRM, engagement, enrichment, forecasting, coaching, CPQ, etc.) costing $500K-$2M+ annually. Data lives in silos, workflows require manual glue, and sellers spend more time on admin than selling. The problem intensifies as AI capabilities require unified data to function effectively -- you cannot run intelligent agents across 15 siloed systems.

**TAM/SAM/SOM**:
- **TAM**: The global CRM market is ~$90B (2025), projected to reach $304B by 2035 at 12.9% CAGR. AuraSell's addressable market extends beyond CRM to include sales engagement (~$5B), conversation intelligence (~$3B), data enrichment (~$4B), CPQ (~$3B), and forecasting tools -- bringing the total addressable category to $100B+. [Sources: Precedence Research, Mordor Intelligence]
- **SAM**: Mid-market and enterprise companies with 50+ sellers using fragmented GTM stacks. Estimated at ~$30-40B.
- **SOM**: Initial target of companies willing to adopt AI-native CRM or overlay. Realistically $1-3B in the near term given adoption curves.

**Market dynamics**: The CRM market is undergoing a generational platform shift driven by AI. Salesforce reported 114% growth in AI/Data Cloud ARR to $2.9B, validating that buyers are spending on AI-enhanced CRM. However, Salesforce's approach is additive (bolting AI onto legacy architecture) rather than native, creating an architectural opening for new entrants. The broader "tool consolidation" trend is real -- CFOs are cutting SaaS spend, and CROs want fewer vendors.

**Timing -- Why now?**:
1. LLM capabilities (GPT-4, Claude) reached production quality in 2023-2024, enabling truly agentic workflows
2. Enterprise AI budgets are expanding rapidly (Salesforce AI ARR growth proves this)
3. CFO pressure to consolidate SaaS spend creates buying motivation
4. Legacy CRM architectures were not designed for AI-first data models

**Direct competitors**:

**Indirect competitors**: Microsoft Dynamics 365 + Copilot, ServiceNow CRM (launched May 2025), Oracle NetSuite, SAP.

**Competitive moat**: AuraSell's potential moat is architectural -- an AI-native data model purpose-built for agentic workflows vs. AI layered atop 20-year-old schemas. If the thesis is correct that AI-native architecture delivers materially better outcomes, this becomes a durable advantage. The GTM OS approach also creates a Trojan horse strategy: land as an overlay, expand as usage grows, eventually replace the core CRM.

**Switching costs**: High once adopted. CRM migration is notoriously painful (Salesforce retention rates exceed 90%). AuraSell benefits from this in retention but suffers from it in acquisition. The GTM OS mitigates acquisition friction by not requiring immediate migration.

## Risks

**1. Salesforce's AI response (Severity: High)**
Salesforce is investing aggressively in Agentforce ($2.9B AI ARR, 114% growth) and making acquisitions to fill architectural gaps. If Salesforce delivers "good enough" AI within its existing platform, the motivation to migrate diminishes significantly. *Mitigation*: AuraSell's GTM OS approach works alongside Salesforce rather than requiring replacement, and architectural advantages of AI-native design may persist.

**2. Unproven at enterprise scale (Severity: High)**
Named customers (AVO Automation, MobiClocks) are mid-market. Enterprise sales cycles are 6-18 months, require SOC 2/ISO compliance, complex integrations, and reference customers. No evidence yet of enterprise traction. *Mitigation*: The team has extensive enterprise experience. David Winslow (ex-Salesforce Office of CEO) was likely hired specifically for enterprise credibility.

**3. Unit economics and AI cost structure (Severity: Medium-High)**
41M agent runs and 870M contact records imply substantial AI inference and data licensing costs. If gross margins are 50-60% rather than SaaS-typical 75-80%, the company will need significantly more capital to reach profitability. *Mitigation*: AI inference costs are declining rapidly. Tool consolidation pricing allows premium capture.

**4. Execution complexity (Severity: Medium)**
Replacing 15+ tools means building 15 products that are each "good enough" to compete with best-of-breed alternatives. Feature breadth risks come at the cost of depth. *Mitigation*: AI can close the feature gap vs. legacy tools since much of the value shifts from UI to intelligence. CPQ, dialer, and enrichment shipped within three months.

**5. Market timing and crowding (Severity: Medium)**
Attio ($52M Series B, 5,000 customers), ServiceNow CRM, and other AI-native entrants are attacking the same opportunity. Multiple well-funded startups pursuing the same thesis increases competition for customers and talent. *Mitigation*: AuraSell's full-stack approach and GTM OS overlay differentiate from Attio's developer-focused CRM.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull case**:
1. **Generational platform shift**: CRM is undergoing its biggest disruption since cloud. AI-native architecture has a structural advantage over 20-year-old Salesforce schemas, similar to how Salesforce disrupted Siebel.
2. **Exceptional founder-market fit**: Eubanks personally lived this problem across five hypergrowth companies. The team's operating experience (Twilio IPO, Harness $1M-to-$100M, Meraki-to-Cisco) is rare for seed-stage founders.
3. **GTM OS as a Trojan horse**: The overlay approach dramatically reduces adoption friction, creates a land-and-expand path, and avoids the "rip and replace Salesforce" death sentence.
4. **Tool consolidation tailwind**: CFOs are cutting SaaS spend. A platform that replaces 5-7 vendors while adding AI is aligned with buyer priorities.
5. **Exceptional seed round signal**: $40M oversubscribed, $25M in 12 hours, N47+Menlo+Unusual is a strong syndicate for enterprise SaaS.

**Bear case**:
1. **Salesforce is not Siebel**: Salesforce has $35B+ revenue, massive distribution, and is spending billions on AI. They can acquire their way out of architectural limitations (and are doing so).
2. **No evidence of revenue**: Seven months post-launch with no disclosed ARR. Only two named mid-market customers. The "41M agent runs" metric may be a vanity metric if it does not translate to revenue.
3. **AI cost structure may kill margins**: Running hundreds of millions of agent runs on LLMs with 870M contact enrichment could produce gross margins that make this business uneconomic without massive scale.
4. **CRM replacement is a graveyard**: History is littered with CRM challengers (Freshworks, Pipedrive, Close) that hit growth ceilings. Enterprise buyers default to Salesforce for career safety.
5. **Crowded AI-native CRM space**: Attio has 5,000 customers and is growing 4x. ServiceNow entered CRM. The window for differentiation may be narrowing.

**What must be true for 10x+ return**:
1. AI-native architecture delivers measurably superior outcomes (not just incremental improvement) vs. AI-augmented legacy CRM
2. AuraSell achieves $5M+ ARR within 18 months and demonstrates enterprise customer wins (not just mid-market)
3. Salesforce's Agentforce stumbles or proves insufficient, leaving an opening for 2-3 years
4. Unit economics prove SaaS-like (70%+ gross margins) despite AI inference and data costs
5. GTM OS creates a viable land-and-expand motion that converts overlay users to full CRM migration
