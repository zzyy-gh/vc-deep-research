# AuraSell — IC Review

## The Hard Questions

**1. What is the actual ARR today, and is the "41 million agent runs" number real revenue or a vanity metric?**

Why it matters: Seven months post-launch with $30M raised and no disclosed ARR is a yellow flag. "Agent runs" is a proxy metric that could be generated by a handful of pilots on free tiers. If the honest answer is "under $500K ARR," the burn multiple is already catastrophic (20x+), and the clock to Series A starts now regardless of runway math.

What research shows: No ARR disclosed. Estimated $150K–$1.2M based on two named customers and pricing. The financial analysis was forced to model from first principles. That opacity at this stage, for this round size, is unusual.

---

**2. Why did the GTM OS pivot happen six months in?**

Why it matters: The original positioning was explicit — AuraSell replaces Salesforce. If the pivot to "overlay that works on top of Salesforce" happened in February 2026, either (a) enterprise pipeline stalled on the CRM migration objection or (b) the company was pragmatically reducing friction. Both are rational but have different implications. (a) means the core thesis is already encountering market resistance. (b) means the founding CRM replacement narrative was marketing, and the real business is a GTM tooling layer.

What research shows: Product analysis interprets it charitably as a "Trojan horse" strategy. First-principles analysis notes it more bluntly: Rox's $1.2B valuation with the wrap approach suggests market prefers wrap-and-extend. The company has not publicly explained the pivot's causation.

---

**3. Can this team actually sell enterprise, and what does the pipeline look like above mid-market?**

Why it matters: Jason Eubanks has an outstanding enterprise GTM resume (Twilio, Harness, Meraki). But the named customers — AVO Automation and MobiClocks — are small. Enterprise CRM deals take 6–18 months, require SOC 2, multiple champions, and procurement review. There is no evidence of a single enterprise pilot with a company above 500 employees. The $30M seed would imply enterprise was the plan. The traction indicates mid-market is what's actually happening.

What research shows: David Winslow (ex-Salesforce SVP) was hired February 2026 specifically for enterprise credibility. Absence of SOC 2 certification is called out in product analysis as a gating factor. Enterprise pipeline is entirely unconfirmed.

---

**4. What are actual gross margins, including AI inference and data licensing costs?**

Why it matters: This is the variable that determines whether AuraSell is a 75%-margin SaaS business or a 50%-margin AI services business. The difference is enormous at scale — a 25-point margin compression on $50M ARR is $12.5M in forgone cash, an entire funding round. Clay and comparable AI-native GTM companies have faced this squeeze. If gross margins are 55–60%, the business needs to raise substantially more capital and the return math changes.

What research shows: 41 million agent runs suggests significant inference spend. At $0.01 per complex action, that is $410K in API costs alone — against what may be sub-$1M ARR. Financial analysis flags this explicitly. No gross margin data has been disclosed. This is the single most important financial diligence item.

---

**5. Is the "AI-native architecture" advantage real and defensible, or is it narrative?**

Why it matters: The Salesforce-is-Siebel analogy is compelling until you stress-test it. Siebel could not adopt cloud because cloud was a deployment model that required rewriting the entire stack. Salesforce is adopting AI on top of an existing cloud infrastructure. The architectural gap is much narrower. Agentforce already has $500M+ ARR and 10,000+ deals closed. If Salesforce's AI is "good enough" for 80% of buyers, the architectural purity argument never translates into commercial advantage.

What research shows: First-principles analysis is direct — "Salesforce can adopt AI much more easily than Siebel could adopt cloud." Product analysis notes Salesforce Agentforce Contact Center launched March 2026. The incumbent is not standing still, and it has 202,600 customers and $35B in revenue as its distribution engine.

---

**6. What is the data moat, really? Who owns the 870 million contacts?**

Why it matters: The "85M company records, 870M contacts" asset is central to the product pitch. If these are licensed from Apollo, ZoomInfo, or Bombora, then AuraSell has no proprietary data advantage — every competitor can access the same data. More critically, if ZoomInfo or Apollo restricts access to AI-native CRM competitors, this feature collapses. Data enrichment is core to the platform's differentiation in demos.

What research shows: Research notes the data source is undisclosed. Product analysis explicitly flags third-party data dependency as a key risk. First-principles analysis notes this is "buy vs. build" infrastructure, not a genuine moat. The honest characterization is: AuraSell has assembled third-party data at scale, not built a proprietary database.

---

**7. How does this win the mid-market horse race against Rox, Attio, Day AI, and Clarify?**

Why it matters: There are at minimum 10–15 well-funded AI-native CRM/GTM startups pursuing the same mid-market RevOps buyer simultaneously. Rox ($1.2B valuation, Sequoia/GC), Attio ($116M raised, 5,000 customers, 4x ARR growth), Day AI (Sequoia Series A), Clarify ($22.5M), Monaco (Founders Fund). Capital is not a differentiator here. AuraSell's answer needs to be specific and defensible, not "we cover more of the stack."

What research shows: AuraSell's differentiation pitch is breadth (15+ tool replacement) and GTM OS overlay. The breadth argument is double-edged — it means every module is shallower than a specialist at launch. No customer count or ARR data allows any competitive benchmarking. Attio's 5,000 customers and 4x growth while AuraSell has two named customers is the current scoreboard.

---

**8. What happens at Series A if ARR is under $3M?**

Why it matters: The implied seed valuation is $120–$150M post-money. A credible Series A step-up to $300–$500M requires $3–5M ARR at 60–100x revenue multiples, which are current AI SaaS premiums that could compress. If the company reaches Series A at $1M ARR and a $150M ask, that is a down-round scenario or a bridge-round scenario. Both are damaging to cap table dynamics and team morale, and both complicate any follow-on participation by current investors.

What research shows: Financial analysis estimates a Series A window in Q4 2026/Q1 2027. The burn rate will increase with the CMO and VP RevOps hires and subsequent go-to-market scaling. There is a 12–18 month window to demonstrate the ARR trajectory that justifies the step-up. The company has not disclosed a fundraising plan or ARR target.

---

**9. Is the exit path M&A or IPO, and at what valuation?**

Why it matters: A $150M seed valuation requires a $1.5B+ exit to return 10x for seed investors. In enterprise SaaS, the M&A comp set (Outreach rumored at $1.5B — below peak — Sana at $1.1B to Workday) suggests the M&A ceiling is $1–3B for a category leader in sales tech that is not yet independent. An IPO requires $150M+ ARR with strong growth and clean unit economics — a 5–7 year timeline at minimum. The path to fund-returning outcome is narrower than the narrative implies.

What research shows: First-principles analysis concludes the most likely positive outcome is a $500M–$2B acquisition. Freshworks analog is instructive — built a real business, IPO'd, never threatened Salesforce, market cap compressed. CRM challengers almost universally get acquired or plateau. This is the pattern, not the exception.

---

**10. What is Salesforce's realistic ability to bundle AuraSell's core value proposition away?**

Why it matters: Salesforce's Agentic Enterprise License Agreement (AELA) is already structured to prevent churn to AI-native challengers by bundling Agentforce capabilities into existing Enterprise licenses. If Salesforce offers "good enough" AI automation at no additional cost, the cost-savings argument — AuraSell's most concrete and measurable customer value proposition — disappears overnight. The company's ability to survive a Salesforce bundle response is the central survivability question.

What research shows: First-principles analysis calls this the highest-likelihood threat. Salesforce has 10,000+ paid Agentforce deals and $500M+ Agentforce ARR as of early 2026. The AELA structure was designed explicitly for this. AuraSell's GTM OS response (sit atop Salesforce rather than replacing it) is strategically clever but also means Salesforce can see exactly what workflow gaps AuraSell is filling and copy them.

## Returns Analysis

**Entry valuation**: The implied post-money seed valuation of $120–$150M is at the top 1% of seed rounds historically. It is justified by team pedigree and round dynamics (28-hour oversubscribed close), not by revenue or customer metrics. At this price, the bar for returns is high. There is no margin of safety here — you are paying for optionality on an exceptional team in a large market.

**Path to 10x**: For a fund investing at $120–$150M post-money, a 10x return requires a $1.2–1.5B+ exit or valuation at point of secondary liquidity. In the CRM/GTM space, this means: $80–100M+ ARR at IPO or being the top-1 or top-2 player acquired by a strategic (Salesforce, Workday, SAP, Oracle, Microsoft). The path is: mid-market traction by end of 2026 ($3–5M ARR), credible enterprise wins by 2027, Series B at $500M+ valuation, acquisition or late-stage financing at $1B+ by 2028–2029. Each step requires execution without missteps in a crowded competitive environment. Plausible, but not probable. Assign 15–25% probability to the 10x path.

**Exit scenarios**:
- *Bear*: Company plateaus at $10–20M ARR, mid-market traction without enterprise breakthrough, raises a flat/down Series B, acquired by a strategic for $200–500M (1.5–3x on seed, not a fund returner)
- *Base*: Company becomes a mid-market RevOps leader, reaches $40–60M ARR, acquired for $600M–$1.2B (4–8x on seed, respectable but not exceptional)
- *Bull*: AuraSell becomes the category-defining AI-native GTM platform, reaches $100M+ ARR, IPOs or is acquired for $2B+ (13–17x on seed, genuine fund returner)

**Time to exit**: 5–8 years to IPO-scale outcome. 3–5 years to likely M&A range. The 28-hour seed close implies investor belief in the upper tail, but base case holding period is 6–7 years.

**Follow-on**: The company will almost certainly raise a Series A within 18 months. Budget for 2–3x follow-on capital (Series A + potential B participation) to maintain ownership. Total capital deployed to maintain pro-rata through Series B could be 3–4x the initial check. Pro-rata is essential here — if the bull case plays out, dilution through Series A and B will compress fund economics unless you exercise rights.

## Fund Fit Assessment

**Stage fit**: Seed is within mandate for most multi-stage funds. However, the $120–150M seed valuation means this is pricing more like a Series A. If your fund writes $3–5M checks, you are getting 2–4% ownership at entry. After Series A and B dilution, you arrive at the exit with 1–2% — which means you need a $1B+ outcome to return even 1–2x on a $10M fund. The math only works if you have pro-rata rights and a fund size large enough to exercise them.

**Check size**: A $3–5M check at $120–150M post-money buys 2–4% ownership. To return a $500M fund 1x on this deal alone, you need a $5B+ exit at 2% ownership. This deal can contribute meaningfully to fund returns but cannot carry the fund unless initial ownership plus follow-on gets to 5%+.

**Portfolio conflict**: Check existing portfolio for: AI-native GTM/CRM tools, RevOps automation, sales intelligence platforms. The space is crowded enough that conflicts are likely at any active enterprise SaaS fund. Resolve before proceeding.

**Thesis alignment**: Strong fit for funds with an AI infrastructure or enterprise SaaS transformation thesis. This is a core AI-era horizontal infrastructure bet — if you believe AI is rewriting enterprise workflows and not just augmenting them, AuraSell fits the thesis precisely.

**Value-add**: The fund's contribution matters most on two dimensions: (1) enterprise customer introductions (CRO, VP Sales, RevOps at $100M–$1B revenue companies) and (2) Series A syndicate relationships. If you have a portfolio of enterprise software companies or connections to the RevOps buyer community, that is genuinely valuable to a company at this stage. Operational support on GTM is high-leverage given the company's immediate challenge.

## What's Missing from the Memo

**Critical before committing**:
- Actual ARR and customer count (even under NDA). This is non-negotiable. Any investor writing a check without this number is flying blind.
- Gross margin breakdown: AI inference cost per agent run, data licensing monthly cost, net gross margin percentage.
- GTM OS pipeline: How many GTM OS pilots vs. full CRM replacement deals? What is the average deal size and sales cycle length for each?
- SOC 2 status: Is it in progress? Timeline to certification? Enterprise deals stall without this.
- Cap table and actual post-money from the seed round (not estimated).

**Diligence calls required**:
- *Customer references*: Jason Eubanks at AVO Automation and MobiClocks — go beyond the press release to understand: what did onboarding look like, what broke, would they recommend it to peers?
- *Former employees*: VMware, Nutanix, Harness colleagues of Srinivas Bandi — technical architecture assessment from people who've built enterprise platforms with him.
- *Competitive intelligence call*: Talk to a RevOps leader at a 100–500 person company currently evaluating AuraSell alongside Attio, Rox, or Salesforce Agentforce. What is the real buying process?
- *Domain expert*: Former Salesforce VP of Product on the technical feasibility of the AI-native architecture advantage claim. Is it real or narrative?
- *Data licensing due diligence*: Who are the actual data providers for the 870M contacts? What are the contract terms, exclusivity provisions (none expected), and data quality SLAs?

**Data room items**:
- Board deck and financial model (even preliminary)
- Customer contracts for AVO Automation and MobiClocks — actual ACV and contract structure
- AI inference cost breakdown by product line
- SOC 2 readiness assessment
- Technical architecture document

## Comparison to Passed Deals

This deal pattern — "Salesforce killer with AI" — has appeared in IC rooms repeatedly since 2020. The typical pass scenario: strong founders, large market, compelling demo, narrative-driven seed round, followed by a Series A with weak metrics (sub-$2M ARR after 18 months) because enterprise sales cycles are longer than anticipated and the CRM migration objection proves more durable than the pitch implied. The companies that fit this pattern include multiple stealth-mode enterprise AI companies from 2021–2023 that are now quietly looking for buyers.

**What separates AuraSell from the consensus pass**:
- Eubanks' specific operator background is rarer than typical founder pedigree claims. He personally scaled Harness from $1M to $100M ARR — that is a relevant proof point for the specific capability needed here.
- The GTM OS pivot is smarter than most "Salesforce killers" have been. It reduces the migration objection while maintaining strategic optionality.
- N47 (Siemens-affiliated), Menlo, and Unusual is a more enterprise-sophisticated syndicate than the typical AI hype round. These investors have seen CRM deals fail before.
- The 28-hour oversubscribed close is not fake signal — sophisticated enterprise SaaS investors do not move that fast on bad deals.

**Reason to pause before the check**:
Two named mid-market customers and no ARR seven months in. If this were a consumer company or a bottoms-up PLG business, that might be acceptable. For a company targeting enterprise sales teams with a full-stack replacement thesis, this early traction is thin relative to the capital raised and the valuation.

---

## IC Recommendation

**Conditional interest**: Proceed if current ARR exceeds $1M with at least one enterprise pilot (500+ employee company) in the pipeline, gross margins are confirmed at 65%+ net of AI inference costs, and SOC 2 is in process with a defined completion date.

The team is genuinely differentiated — Eubanks' Harness trajectory is the closest real analog to what AuraSell is attempting to do, and the GTM OS pivot shows founder sophistication rather than stubbornness. The market timing is real. However, the valuation is aggressive for the demonstrated traction, and writing a check today means accepting that the core financial diligence questions are unresolved. The right move is to get the actual numbers under NDA before committing. If the ARR trajectory and gross margin profile hold up to scrutiny, this is worth a term sheet conversation. If ARR is below $500K with no enterprise pipeline visibility, pass and revisit in 90 days when the data is cleaner.
