# AuraSell — Quick Screen

## One-Liner
AI-native CRM and GTM platform that consolidates 15+ sales tools into a single system with agentic workflows.

## Key Facts
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Founded | August 2025 |
| HQ | San Francisco, CA |
| Stage | Seed |
| Last Round | $30M seed, closed within 28 hours (N47, Menlo Ventures, Unusual Ventures) |
| Total Raised | $30M |
| Employees | ~40 |
| Sector | Enterprise SaaS / Sales Operations / RevOps |

## What They Do
AuraSell is an AI-native platform that replaces fragmented sales stacks by consolidating tools like Salesforce, quoting software, territory management, and forecasting into a single unified system. The platform uses AI agents to automate repetitive sales workflows (call analysis, deal routing, proposal generation) while maintaining a system of record. Target buyer is mid-market to enterprise sales and RevOps teams.

## Team Snapshot
**Jason Eubanks** (CEO) — Former operator at Twilio, Harness, and other hypergrowth companies. **Srinivas Bandi** (CTO) — Ex-VMware, Nutanix, Meraki; deep infrastructure and platform background. Both co-founders have successful GTM/operations experience.

## Traction Signal
Pre-revenue or early adoption (not disclosed publicly). Early-stage traction: 41 million AI agent runs in the first few weeks post-launch. Platform ships with 85M company records and 850M contacts. Strong investor velocity (seed round oversubscribed, closed in 28 hours with majority of capital committed in first 12 hours). No public revenue or customer count disclosed yet.

## Market Context
TAM is substantial: Salesforce $30B+ TAM, plus fragmented point solutions (Outreach, Clari, etc.). Timing is favorable with AI agent adoption accelerating and enterprise desire to reduce tool sprawl. AuraSell positions itself as reducing GTM SaaS spend by ~50% while improving productivity 250x. Competitive landscape includes Salesforce (with Agentforce), Microsoft (Dynamics 365 + Copilot), emerging AI-native challengers like Twelve Labs (Retool competitor), and point-solution players.

## Red Flags
None identified in quick screen. Note: Company launched from stealth just 6 months ago (August 2025), so track record is extremely limited. No public revenue, customer names, or usage metrics beyond agent run counts. Execution risk on delivering promised tool consolidation at scale is material. Strong macro headwinds (CRM market saturated, AI-native claims need to be validated).

## Initial Take
**Proceed to deeper research.** Strong founding team (proven operator + technical depth), hot market (AI, sales operations), exceptional seed round reception (28-hour close, partial oversubscribe) are compelling signals. However, company is extremely new (6 months), unproven product-market fit, and makes bold claims about replacing Salesforce. Recommend diving into: (1) actual customer adoption and retention, (2) product differentiation vs. legacy vendors adding AI, (3) unit economics assumptions, (4) go-to-market and land-and-expand strategy.
